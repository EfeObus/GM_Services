Loan Privacy Policy

How we collect, use, and protect your personal information in compliance with Nigerian data protection laws

This policy complies with the Nigeria Data Protection Regulation (NDPR) 2019

1. Introduction and Scope

1.1 Our Commitment

GM Services Nigeria Limited ("GM Services", "we", "us", "our") is committed to protecting the privacy and personal data of our loan applicants and customers ("you", "your", "data subject"). This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, share, and protect your personal information in connection with our loan services within the Federal Republic of Nigeria.

1.2 Legal Basis

This Privacy Policy is established in accordance with:

  • Nigeria Data Protection Regulation (NDPR) 2019
  • Constitution of the Federal Republic of Nigeria 1999
  • Central Bank of Nigeria regulations on data protection
  • Banks and Other Financial Institutions Act (BOFIA) 2020
  • Consumer Protection Council Act 2019
  • Evidence Act 2011 (Electronic transactions)
Your Rights Under NDPR

As a Nigerian data subject, you have specific rights regarding your personal data including access, rectification, erasure, portability, and objection to processing. These rights are detailed in Section 6 of this policy.

  • Application assessment and verification
  • Credit scoring and risk evaluation
  • Loan approval and disbursement
  • Account setup and management
  • Repayment tracking and collections
Risk Management
  • Fraud detection and prevention
  • Anti-money laundering (AML) checks
  • Know Your Customer (KYC) compliance
  • Credit bureau reporting
  • Portfolio risk monitoring
3.2 Legal Basis for Processing
Processing Purpose Legal Basis Justification
Loan Processing Contractual Necessity Required to fulfill loan agreement and provide services
Credit Assessment Legitimate Interest Necessary for lending decision and risk management
KYC/AML Compliance Legal Obligation Required by CBN and FMFBNP regulations

Debt Recovery Legitimate Interest Protection of legal rights and debt collection
3.3 Secondary Uses
  • Service Improvement: Analytics to enhance loan products and user experience
  • Customer Support: Resolving queries and providing assistance
  • Marketing: Promoting relevant financial products (with consent)
  • Research: Anonymous data for market research and product development
  • Legal Compliance: Meeting regulatory reporting requirements

4.2 Data Sharing Safeguards
  • All third parties must sign comprehensive data processing agreements
  • Data sharing is limited to the minimum necessary for the specific purpose
  • Third parties must implement appropriate technical and organizational measures
  • Regular audits and compliance monitoring of data processors
  • Immediate notification of any data security incidents
Prohibited Data Sharing

We will never sell, rent, or trade your personal data to third parties for marketing purposes. We do not share data with unauthorized parties or for purposes outside those disclosed in this policy.

5. Data Security and Protection Measures

5.1 Technical Security Measures
Encryption and Access Control
  • 256-bit SSL/TLS encryption for data transmission
  • AES-256 encryption for data at rest
  • Multi-factor authentication for system access
  • Role-based access control and least privilege principle
  • Regular access reviews and deprovisioning
Infrastructure Security
  • Secure cloud hosting with Nigerian data residency
  • Firewall protection and intrusion detection
  • Regular security patches and updates
  • Continuous monitoring and threat detection
  • Disaster recovery and business continuity plans
5.2 Organizational Security Measures
Security Area Measures Implemented
Staff Training Mandatory data protection training, confidentiality agreements, background checks
Access Management Need-to-know basis, regular access audits, immediate revocation upon termination
Physical Security Secure facilities, visitor controls, locked storage, CCTV monitoring
Incident Response 24/7 security operations center, incident response plan, breach notification procedures
Compliance Monitoring Regular security audits, penetration testing, compliance assessments
5.3 Data Breach Response

In the event of a personal data breach, we will:

  1. Contain and assess the breach within 24 hours
  2. Notify the Nigeria Data Protection Commission within 72 hours
  3. Inform affected individuals within 72 hours if high risk to rights and freedoms
  4. Provide clear information about the nature and impact of the breach
  5. Implement measures to mitigate adverse effects
  6. Document the incident and lessons learned for future prevention

7. Data Retention and Deletion

7.1 Retention Periods
Data Category Retention Period Legal Basis and Purpose

7.2 Secure Deletion Process

When retention periods expire, we ensure secure deletion through:

  • Automated deletion schedules based on retention policies
  • Multi-pass overwriting for digital data destruction
  • Physical destruction of paper documents by certified vendors
  • Certificate of destruction for compliance verification
  • Regular audits to ensure deletion compliance
Retention Exceptions

Data may be retained beyond standard periods if required for ongoing legal proceedings, regulatory investigations, or legitimate business purposes as permitted by law.

8. Cross-Border Data Transfers

8.1 Data Residency Commitment

GM Services is committed to keeping Nigerian customer data within Nigeria in compliance with NDPR requirements. Our primary data centers are located in Nigeria with local service providers.

8.2 Limited International Transfers

In limited circumstances, we may transfer data internationally for:

  • Technical support and maintenance by verified vendors
  • Cloud backup services with Nigerian data protection addendum
  • International credit checks for foreign income verification
  • Legal compliance in multi-jurisdictional matters
8.3 Transfer Safeguards
Legal Protection
  • NDPR-compliant data processing agreements
  • Adequate data protection certification
  • Standard contractual clauses
  • Explicit consent where required
Technical Protection
  • End-to-end encryption during transfer
  • Secure transmission protocols
  • Access logging and monitoring
  • Return or deletion guarantees
